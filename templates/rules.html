University Dormitory Rulebook

A guide to maintaining a safe, respectful, and supportive dormitory community.

The university dormitory exists to provide students with a safe, clean, respectful, and supportive living environment that promotes academic success, discipline, and community responsibility.

  • Only currently enrolled students of the university are allowed to reside in the dormitory.
  • Residents must submit all required documents and follow the dorm application and renewal process.
  • Room assignments are non-transferable unless approved by dorm management.

  • Residents must show respect to fellow residents, dorm staff, and university property.
  • Any form of harassment, bullying, threats, or violence is strictly prohibited.
  • Disorderly conduct, vandalism, or intentional damage to property is not allowed.
  • Residents must follow university rules in addition to dormitory rules.

Quiet hours are enforced to support rest and study:

  • Weekdays: 10:00 PM – 6:00 AM
  • Weekends: 11:00 PM – 7:00 AM

Loud music, shouting, or disruptive activities during quiet hours are prohibited. Earphones are recommended.

  • Visitors are allowed only during designated visiting hours.
  • All visitors must sign the visitor logbook.
  • Overnight guests require official approval.
  • Residents are responsible for their guests’ behavior.

  • Residents must keep rooms clean and organized.
  • Dispose trash properly in designated areas.
  • Cleaning groups rotate daily for common areas.
  • Failure to follow schedules may result in penalties.
  • Report damages immediately to dorm staff.

  • Illegal drugs, vaping devices, and alcohol
  • Weapons or dangerous items
  • Candles or open flames
  • Gambling or illegal activities
  • Pets (except approved service animals)

  • Carry your Dorm ID at all times.
  • Do not share keys or access cards.
  • Tampering with fire safety equipment is a serious offense.
  • Follow curfew policies if applicable.

  • Use common areas responsibly.
  • Do not leave personal belongings unattended.
  • Misuse of facilities may lead to restricted access.

  • Dorm internet should be used responsibly.
  • Downloading illegal content is prohibited.
  • Conserve electricity and water whenever possible.

  • First offense: Warning
  • Second offense: Community service or penalty
  • Third offense: Suspension or removal

Serious violations may result in immediate eviction.

  • Follow evacuation procedures during emergencies.
  • Keep emergency exits accessible.
  • Report emergencies immediately to staff or security.

The dormitory administration reserves the right to amend this rulebook. Residents will be notified of any updates.
