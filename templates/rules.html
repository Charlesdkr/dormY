University Dormitory Rulebook

A guide to a safe, respectful, and supportive community living environment.

The university dormitory exists to provide students with a safe, clean, respectful, and supportive living environment that promotes academic success, discipline, and community responsibility.

  • Only currently enrolled students of the university are allowed to reside in the dormitory.
  • Residents must submit all required documents and follow the dorm application and renewal process.
  • Room assignments are non-transferable unless approved by dorm management.

  • Residents must show respect to fellow residents, dorm staff, and university property.
  • Any form of harassment, bullying, threats, or violence is strictly prohibited.
  • Disorderly conduct, vandalism, or intentional damage to property is not allowed.
  • Residents must follow university rules in addition to dormitory rules.

Quiet hours are enforced to support rest and study:

  • Weekdays: 10:00 PM – 6:00 AM
  • Weekends: 11:00 PM – 7:00 AM

Loud music, shouting, or disruptive activities during quiet hours are prohibited. Use of speakers is discouraged; earphones are recommended.

  • Visitors are allowed only during designated visiting hours.
  • All visitors must sign the visitor logbook at the entrance.
  • Overnight guests are not allowed unless officially approved.
  • Residents are responsible for the behavior of their guests.

  • Residents must keep their rooms clean and organized at all times.
  • Trash must be disposed of properly in designated areas.
  • Assigned cleaning groups will rotate daily for common areas (hallways, comfort rooms, lobby).
  • Failure to follow cleaning schedules may result in warnings or penalties.
  • Any damages should be reported immediately to dorm staff.

The following are strictly prohibited inside the dormitory:

  • Illegal drugs, vaping devices, and alcohol
  • Weapons or sharp objects not intended for school use
  • Candles, open flames, or cooking appliances inside rooms
  • Gambling and illegal activities
  • Pets (except approved service animals)

  • Residents must carry their Dorm ID at all times.
  • Sharing of room keys or access cards is prohibited.
  • Tampering with fire alarms, extinguishers, or CCTV is a serious offense.
  • Curfew policies, if applicable, must be strictly followed.

  • Common areas must be used responsibly and left clean after use.
  • Personal belongings should not be left unattended in shared spaces.
  • Any misuse of facilities may result in restricted access.

  • Dorm internet is for academic and responsible personal use.
  • Downloading illegal content is prohibited.
  • Residents should conserve electricity and water whenever possible.

Violations will be handled as follows:

  • First offense: Verbal or written warning
  • Second offense: Community service or penalty
  • Third offense: Suspension or removal from dormitory

Serious violations may result in immediate eviction.

  • In case of fire, earthquake, or emergencies, residents must follow evacuation protocols.
  • Emergency exits must always remain accessible.
  • Report emergencies immediately to dorm staff or security.

The dormitory administration reserves the right to amend or update this rulebook as necessary. Residents will be informed of any changes.
